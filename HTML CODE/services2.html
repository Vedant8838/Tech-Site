• Email Security

Email security refers to the practice of protecting email accounts and communications from unauthorized access, loss, or compromise by implementing various security measures like spam filters, malware detection, encryption, and strong password policies, effectively safeguarding sensitive information transmitted through email.

    Key points about email security:

  • Goal: To prevent malicious actors from accessing, altering, or deleting emails intended for specific recipients.
  • Common threats: Spam emails, phishing attempts, malware attachments, email spoofing.

    • Protective measures:

  • Spam filters: Identify and block unwanted bulk emails
  • Anti-malware scanning: Detect and block malicious attachments
  • Email encryption: Protect sensitive information by scrambling the email content
  • Two-factor authentication: Requires additional verification beyond just a password to access email accounts
  • Secure email gateways: Filter incoming and outgoing emails for potential threats



• Email Archival

Email archiving is the process of storing emails in a secure location for future use. It helps businesses organize and manage large volumes of emails, and comply with regulations.

    Benefits of email archiving

  • Compliance: Organizations in regulated industries may be required to archive emails for a set number of years.
  • Disaster recovery: Email archives can protect against data loss in case of a system failure or disaster.
  • Legal discovery: Email archives can be used to search for emails related to legal requests or subpoenas.
  • Business continuity: Email archives can help businesses maintain continuity in the event of employee turnover.
  • Records management: Email archives can help businesses manage and organize their records.
  • Intellectual property: Email archives can help businesses protect their intellectual property.

Steps in compliance management Identify relevant laws and regulations, Conduct risk analysis, Develop compliance policies, Train employees, and Build a culture of compliance.

• Conditional Access Policy

A conditional access policy is a set of rules that controls access to resources or systems based on conditions. It's a security approach that helps organizations protect their data and applications.

    How it works

  • Conditional access policies use signals from various sources to make access decisions.
  • These signals can include user or group membership, device health, location, and the sensitivity of the accessed resources.
  • Policies can be targeted to specific users and groups.
  • For example, you can create a policy that allows employees to bypass MFA if they log in with a company-compliant device and network.


    • Benefits

  • Conditional access policies help keep organizations secure.
  • They provide centralized control and customization options.
  • They allow you to enforce specific access controls when needed.
